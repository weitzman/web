Schedule a road test

Ready to take a road test before getting your license? You can schedule your road test online.

Quick Actions

  • Schedule your road test online 

The Details

Page Contents

Overview Eligibility Fees How to More info Forms

Overview

A road test is the last step before getting your driver's license. You need to have a valid learner’s permit to schedule a road test. You can schedule your road test for a Class D (passenger vehicle) or Class M (motorcycle) license online.

Eligibility

If you are under 18 and are applying for a Class D (passenger vehicle) license, you must complete a Driver’s Education Program and have a valid learner's permit. 

If you are under 18 and are applying for a Class M (motorcycle) license, you must complete a Massachusetts Rider Education Program (MREP).

Applicants under 18 must complete all Junior Operator License requirements.

If you're over 18, you don't need to complete a Driver’s Education Program or a MREP unless you have failed two motorcycle tests.

Fees

See the Registry of Motor Vehicles (RMV) Schedule of Fees for road test and driver's license fees.

It is best to pay all fees when you schedule your road test so that you will not have to return in person to an RMV office to have your driver's license issued. You can pay your road test and licensing fees online.

How to

To schedule your road test, you must provide your:

  • Name
  • Date of birth 
  • Social Security number
  • Learner’s permit number

There are 2 ways to schedule your test.

The fastest and easiest way to schedule a road test is online.

Schedule your road test online 

You can call the RMV Monday-Friday, 9 a.m.-5 p.m. except holidays, to schedule a road test. The number to call depends on your area code:

  • (857) 368-8000, from Massachusetts area codes 339, 617, 781, 857 or from outside of Massachusetts
  • (800) 858-3926, from all other Massachusetts area codes
  • TTY: (877) 768-8833, for people who are deaf or hard-of-hearing (in Massachusetts)
  • Automated: (857) 368-8010

More info

See the Road Test Information Sheet for a checklist of requirements and other information.

To take your road test, you'll need:

  • A completed road test application. If you're under 18, your parent or guardian must complete the Parental Consent section of the application.
  • A legally registered vehicle to use for your test. Your vehicle must have an active registration (which you must present), and a valid inspection sticker. 
  • See the Road Test Information Sheet for specific requirements for leased and out-of-state registered vehicles.

For a Class D (passenger vehicle) road test, you'll also need to be accompanied by a sponsor who is at least 21 years old and has had a valid U.S. driver's license, issued by his or her home state, for at least one year. The driver's license must be presented to the examiner.

Class M (motorcycle) road test applicants do not require a sponsor.

You can’t take more than 6 Class D (passenger vehicle) road tests in one year.

If you fail 2 Class M (motorcycle) road tests, you must successfully complete beginner motorcycle rider education course (MREP).

If you are under 18, you must complete the MREP to obtain a driver's license. 

If you successfully complete the MREP, you do not need to take a Class M road test.

Road test fees are not refunded if you:

  • Fail the test
  • Are unprepared
  • Do not have a registered vehicle
  • Are late or do not appear
  • Cancel or reschedule your test with less than 72 hours notice

You can also cancel your road test online.
