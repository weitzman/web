Overview

The highlight of Purgatory Chasm State Reservation is its quarter-mile gorge of granite rock walls, which rise up to 70 feet. A number of short trails surround the chasm, offering great views of this unique natural landmark. It is believed that the chasm was created by a surge of glacial meltwater about 14,000 years ago. If you visit, be aware that the chasm rocks and slopes can be slippery. Wear rubber soled shoes or hiking boots, and stay away from the edges of the chasm.

Hours

Chasm

Closed in winter when snow and ice are present
Sunrise to sunset

Parking

Daily parking is $5 for vehicles with Massachusetts license plates, and $6 for vehicles with out-of-state plates.

Recommended activities

Hiking and walking

Six trails that are 1 mile or less lead to various rock formations and picnic areas. Rocks and slopes can be slippery, so wear rubber-soled shoes or hiking boots. Avoid hiking the chasm during or immediately after inclement weather due to extremely slippery rocks.

Additional activities

  • Picnicking
  • Grilling — grills are provided, but visitors are welcome to bring their own charcoal or gas grills
  • Interpretive and educational tours, hikes, and programs

Restrictions

  • Dogs are allowed but must be leashed at all times
  • Alcoholic beverages are not allowed
  • Open fires are not allowed
  • Mountain bikes, bicycles, scooters, and skateboards are not allowed
  • Motorized vehicles are not allowed on trails
  • Do not pick or remove flowers and other plants
